# Walkthrough: When To Stop

## Scenario

A user asks whether a person would definitely have a legal right or liability under a provision. The text is ambiguous and the answer may depend on court interpretation and facts.

## Suggested Skill Sequence

1. `01-orient-the-text-on-jlib`
   Confirm the source state and officiality.
2. `02-find-the-right-version-and-date`
   Confirm that the source even matches the relevant time frame.
3. `04-read-a-provision-as-logic`
   Set out the competing limbs or conditions.
4. `05-apply-definitions-and-cross-references`
   Resolve the obvious dependencies first.
5. `06-apply-non-obvious-background-rules`
   Check only the maintained Jersey defaults if they are triggered.
6. `07-boundary-check-legislation-alone-or-not`
   Stop if the question now depends on facts, case law, customary law, common law, public-law remedies, or court interpretation.

## Safe Outcome

The correct result may be a limited answer: what the provision appears to say, what source state that reading depends on, and why further authority or facts would still be needed.
