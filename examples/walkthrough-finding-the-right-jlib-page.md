# Walkthrough: Finding The Right JLIB Page

## Scenario

A user asks: `What did this Jersey law say in 2021 about tourism accommodation?`

No JLIB link is supplied.

## Suggested Skill Sequence

1. `00-locate-the-right-jlib-page`
   Start from Laws search rather than whole-site search. Search for the likely law or topic, choose the current law page as the hub, and use the point-in-time feature to reach the 2021 version.
2. `01-orient-the-text-on-jlib`
   Record the instrument, source state, officiality, date range shown on the point-in-time page, and whether any special caution arises.
3. `02-find-the-right-version-and-date`
   Confirm that the question is historical and that the selected point-in-time version matches the target period.
4. `03-cite-and-link-jersey-legislation-properly`
   Preserve the provision-level reference and the date range of the point-in-time page.
5. `04-read-a-provision-as-logic`
   Break the selected provision into rule, conditions, and exceptions.
6. `05-apply-definitions-and-cross-references`
   Pull in linked terms, schedules, and internal references as needed.
7. `06-apply-non-obvious-background-rules`
   Use only if one of the maintained triggers is genuinely present.
8. `07-boundary-check-legislation-alone-or-not`
   Separate the legislative reading from any answer that would depend on facts, case law, or wider context.

## Safe Outcome

Use JLIB's legislation-specific navigation first, land on a stable point-in-time page, and only then begin source-state classification and substantive reading.
