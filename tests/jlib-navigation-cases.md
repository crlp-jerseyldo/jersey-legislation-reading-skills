# JLIB Navigation Cases

## Scenario List

1. Choose Laws search rather than homepage search when the task is plainly legislation-specific.
2. Use quotation marks when the exact law title is known.
3. Use Laws-page filters or sorting to narrow a broad legislation search.
4. Use the Current-laws view toggle when the research task depends on primary versus secondary legislation.
5. Route a present-law question to `Current`.
6. Identify a page in `Current Laws` and capture the `Showing the law from ... to Current` range.
7. Distinguish `Current` from `Current point-in-time` inside the same collection.
8. Reach a historical version through the point-in-time feature on a current-law page.
9. Distinguish `Pending` from enacted or current law.
10. Distinguish `Enacted` from official current consolidated text.
11. Check `Enacted` as well when a question is about future amendments not yet in force.
12. Distinguish `Archive (non-current)` from official current material.
13. Distinguish `Repealed` material from law still in force.
14. Recognise that `Translated` laws are unofficial reading aids.
15. Recognise that `Annotated` laws are contextual aids rather than current-law substitutes.
16. Recognise when a current-collection page is actually UK-extended material and needs special caution.
17. Use official search operators appropriately: `AND`, `OR`, `NOT`, `NEAR`, `ONEAR`, `WORDS`, and `*`.
