# JLIB Collections Map

## Purpose

This note explains how a careful reader should think about JLIB collections and related source states before reading substance. It is an operational aid based on public JLIB guidance, not an official platform manual.

## First Question

Before interpreting any provision, ask:

- what collection or source state is this text from?
- is it official?
- what date range does the page itself show?

Those three facts should be recorded before substantive reading begins.

## Core JLIB Source States

### Current

`Current` laws are laws in force today. They include primary legislation and secondary legislation. JLIB says the current collection is updated as soon as possible after a change comes into effect, usually within a day.

Operational consequence:

- use for present-law questions
- check the page statement such as `Showing the law from 11 January 2025 to Current`
- treat the date range shown on the page as part of the answer record
- if the page carries the official statement, treat it as authorised for use in court unless it is extended UK legislation

### Current Point-In-Time

JLIB provides historical versions inside the current collection. JLIB says these point-in-time versions are available back to `1 January 2019`, and each version states the period for which it was in effect.

Operational consequence:

- use for historical questions from `1 January 2019` onward
- record the stated effective period
- treat these versions as authorised for use in court for the period to which they relate, except extended UK legislation

### Enacted

`Enacted` legislation is the text as originally made, without later amendments. JLIB's enacted FAQ says enacted legislation is not up to date.

Operational consequence:

- use for original-text questions
- do not answer a current-law question from enacted text without saying it is not updated
- check the enacted collection for future amendments not yet in force

### Pending

JLIB says pending laws are not yet considered enacted or current and cannot have effect until they are assented to by the King and registered in the Royal Court.

Operational consequence:

- use only for prospective or process questions
- do not treat pending text as current law
- if a user asks whether a change already applies, pending status is a direct caution flag

### Archive (Non-Current)

JLIB says archive laws contain a large mixed collection of versions of laws that are not current or are not official, including translations.

Operational consequence:

- use carefully for older historical work, legacy versions, and supporting context
- do not assume archive material is official
- state the limitation explicitly in any answer or citation

### Repealed

JLIB says repealed laws are legislation that has been repealed by other legislation, or that has lapsed, expired, or otherwise ceased to be in force.

Operational consequence:

- use only when the question is historical or about repeal status
- do not use repealed text to answer a present-law question

### Translated

JLIB says unofficial English translations are available for some French laws in the current collection to help users understand the law. The translated-laws FAQ says English translations are not admissible in court and the original French law must be used.

Operational consequence:

- use as a reading aid only
- do not treat the translation as the authoritative legal text
- if authority matters, return to the French original

### Annotated

JLIB says the annotated collection contains individual laws from the superseded revised edition with article-level annotations about reported cases. JLIB also says keeping annotations updated is complex now that current law is updated as soon as it is amended.

Operational consequence:

- use for case-linking and contextual follow-up
- do not treat annotations as current-law substitutes
- confirm the operative provision in the current or enacted collection as needed

## UK-Extended Material In The Current Collection

JLIB's current-laws FAQ says Orders in Council extending UK legislation to Jersey are in the current collection, but are static and not updated and therefore need to be interpreted with care.

Operational consequence:

- treat `current collection` and `current consolidated Jersey law` as different ideas
- record expressly that the source is UK-extended material
- expect extra caution around scope, modification, and date alignment

## Draft Legislation Outside JLIB

JLIB's public guidance says draft legislation, other than pending laws, is not available on the site. Draft legislation to be debated by the States Assembly is published as a proposition on the States Assembly website.

Operational consequence:

- do not confuse a States Assembly proposition with enacted or current law
- if a user is asking about future or draft change, identify the source as `draft proposition` rather than JLIB legislation
