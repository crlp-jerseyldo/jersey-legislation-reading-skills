# JLIB Collections Map

## Purpose Of This Note

This note gives a practical overview of how to think about JLIB collections when reading Jersey legislation. It is meant as an operational aid, not as an official platform description.

## Core Reading Question

Before interpreting any provision, ask: what kind of JLIB text am I looking at?

## Practical Collection Categories

Readers may encounter collections or materials that are functionally similar to:

- current legislation
- pending legislation
- non-current or historical material
- translated material
- annotated material
- UK legislation as extended or applied to Jersey

Exact labels may vary over time, so the important task is not memorising names but recognising the reading consequences.

## Operational Consequences By Category

### Current

Useful when the task asks what the law is now. Still check whether the relevant provision is fully in force and whether the current view matches the date needed.

### Pending

Useful for change-tracking, but dangerous if mistaken for law already in force. Treat pending material as prospective unless there is a clear basis for saying otherwise.

### Non-current

Useful for historical questions and date-specific analysis. Do not answer a present-law question from a non-current text without making that limitation explicit.

### Translated

Useful as a reading aid. Treat translation status carefully and do not assume it removes the need to check the legally operative text where that matters.

### Annotated

Useful for navigation and context. Do not silently convert annotation into operative legislative text.

### UK-Extended Material

Useful where Jersey law depends on UK legislation extended, applied, or otherwise relevant to Jersey. This often requires extra care about scope, modification, commencement, and whether local instruments or context alter the picture.

## Reader Checklist

- identify the collection or equivalent status marker
- confirm whether the material is current, historical, pending, or supportive only
- note whether special caution is needed before relying on it
- record that status in any answer or citation

If the collection cannot be identified confidently, the reader should say so and proceed cautiously.
